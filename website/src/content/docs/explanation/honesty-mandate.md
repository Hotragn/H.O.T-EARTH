Every project has a rule it will not break. For H.O.T EARTH it is this:

> Real physics and real data, no fake numbers. Anything illustrative is labelled.
> Every asset is licensed and credited.

This page explains what that means and why it is worth the constraint.

## Why an honesty rule at all

A digital twin is persuasive. A smooth globe with glowing cities and confident
figures *looks* authoritative whether or not the numbers are real. That is
exactly the danger. The moment a viewer cannot tell a measurement from a
decoration, the whole thing becomes an aesthetic, not a model.

The honesty mandate exists so that trust is earned by construction, not by
appearance. It is also what makes the project useful to learn from: you can read
any world's code and its notes and see precisely where each number came from.

## What it means in practice

- **Compute or measure, never invent.** Positions come from ephemerides,
  terminators from solar geometry, forecasts from measured data. If a value
  cannot be computed or sourced, it does not appear as if it were real.
- **Label the illustrative.** Some things genuinely cannot be photographed or
  measured (an exoplanet's surface, a black hole's lensing). These are rendered
  as clearly illustrative, in the UI and in the notes, never as data.
- **State the bounds.** Each world's physics note leads with what is real, what
  is illustrative, and where the limits are. Overclaiming is a bug.
- **License and credit everything.** Only clearly usable sources ship, and the
  exact credit line travels with the asset.

## What it costs, and why it is worth it

The rule says no to things: no fabricated "trusted by" logos, no invented
accuracy, no rendering an object that has never been imaged as if it had. Those
noes are the product. A twin you can trust on the hard cases is a twin you can
trust at all.
