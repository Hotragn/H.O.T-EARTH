> - **ILLUSTRATIVE and LABELED:** any rendered galaxy-disk geometry, particle colouring or

>   3D disk appearance drawn from them is our own stylisation.
>
> Second honesty item, load-bearing: **the cosmic-web radial coordinate is redshift-space,
> not true distance.** Distance is computed as cz/H0, so line-of-sight peculiar velocities
> in clusters produce real "fingers-of-God" elongation, and the whole depth scale moves if

## 1. The real catalog (measured, cited)

Ten named galaxies are catalogued in `lib/galaxies.ts` (`GALAXIES`), each with a published
Hubble type, distance (Mly and Mpc), diameter, approximate stellar count and heliocentric
redshift. The Milky Way is the origin (no heliocentric distance). The others are the
Andromeda Galaxy M31 (SA(s)b, ~2.54 Mly), Triangulum M33, the Large and Small Magellanic
Clouds, M87 / Virgo A (the giant elliptical, ~53.5 Mly, tie-in to the Black Holes tab),
the Sombrero M104, the Whirlpool M51, the barred spiral NGC 1300, and Centaurus A / NGC 5128.

**Distances are real and uncertain.** Extragalactic distances come from the cosmic distance
ladder: parallax and eclipsing binaries (used for the LMC, a ladder cornerstone), Cepheid
period-luminosity relations, the tip of the red-giant branch, surface-brightness
fluctuations, the Tully-Fisher relation, and Type Ia supernovae. Different methods disagree
at the ~5 to 15 percent level, and quoted distances change as calibrations improve, so
these are best published values, not exact constants. The tab presents them as measured,
with the method-dependence acknowledged. Diameters and stellar counts are representative
published figures.

## 2. The real redshift catalog for the cosmic web (the key real dataset)

**File:** `public/data/galaxies/cosmic-web.json` (~18,000 rows, ~740 KB), an array of
`{ ra, dec, z }`. **Metadata:** `public/data/galaxies/cosmic-web.meta.json` records the exact
source, query, row count, z-range, fields, license and acknowledgment.

- **Source:** SDSS DR17 `SpecObj`, fetched live 2026-07-21 from the SkyServer SQL service
  (`https://skyserver.sdss.org/dr17/SkyServerWS/SearchTools/SqlSearch`).
- **Query:** an equatorial thin-declination slice, `class='GALAXY'`, `zWarning=0`,
  `z` between 0.005 and 0.15, `dec` between -2 and +2 deg, `ra` between 110 and 270 deg.
  This is the classic SDSS "pie slice" geometry that shows the cosmic web most clearly.
- **Rows:** 32,154 returned; uniformly random-subsampled (seed 42) to 18,000 to keep the
  file small while preserving spatial density. z-range 0.00503 to 0.14999.
- **Rounding:** RA/Dec to 4 decimals, z to 5 decimals.
- **License:** SDSS data are public. Use requires the standard SDSS acknowledgment (in the
  meta file and section 3 below).
- **Citation:** SDSS DR17, Abdurro'uf et al. (2022), ApJS 259, 35.

**Honest statement (load-bearing): this is redshift-space, with fingers-of-God distortion.**
The plotted radial coordinate is a Hubble distance cz/H0, not a directly measured distance.
Peculiar velocities inside galaxy clusters add to the cosmological redshift, so clusters
appear stretched along the line of sight into the classic "fingers of God"; large-scale
infall produces the flattened "Kaiser" effect on the walls. These are real, well-understood
distortions present in every redshift survey. The structure (filaments, walls, voids) is
genuine; the exact radial depth is redshift-space and moves with the assumed H0.

## Rejected / flagged items

- **Presenting the cosmic web as procedural is rejected.** The points are real SDSS
  measured RA/Dec/z; the structure is genuine, not noise.
- **Presenting the redshift-space depth as true distance is rejected.** The radial axis is
  cz/H0 (redshift-space), fingers-of-God distortion is present and real, and the depth
  scale moves with the assumed H0. Labeled as such.
- **Presenting the named-galaxy distances as exact is rejected.** They are best published
  ladder measurements with real method-dependent uncertainty (~5 to 15 percent).
- **Shipping any image whose license could not be verified is rejected.** All five shipped
  only because CC BY 4.0 was confirmed live against the ESA/Hubble and ESA/Webb policy and
  the individual image pages.
- **Two intended IDs were replaced after live checks:** the first Sombrero candidate
  (heic0307a) turned out to be the Helix Nebula and was replaced with the correct Sombrero
  mosaic opo0328a; a mislabelled deep-field candidate was replaced with the standalone SMACS
  0723 image weic2209a. Identities were confirmed on each image page before shipping.
- **Inventing catalog numbers is rejected.** Every distance, type and redshift is a cited
  measured value.
